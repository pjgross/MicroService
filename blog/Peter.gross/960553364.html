Peter gross : 2) Organised around business capabilities

Created by Peter Gross, last modified on Sep 15, 2020


In a traditional monilith development the complexity of the development means that the teams cover layers of the stack, so you have Front end developers, API specialists, Backend specialists and DBA's that deliver the system.

This can lead to miscommunication and teams working to different objectives, that mean the business capabilities are often delayed.


Traditional projects have teams with horizontal responsibilities

In Microservices, a business capbility is developed by one team, all working to the same objectives and deliver code faster (theoretically)

It is often better to have people that specialise in one area but are capable of working cross multiple, so teams are smaller and more focussed.

In my example app, I have divided the application into tickets (creating inventory), orders, payments and authorisation to set the business capabilities i wanted, so yep one teamÂ 


Motivation

  • Quick development
  • Well-defined boundaries
