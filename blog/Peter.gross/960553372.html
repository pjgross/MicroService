Peter gross : 3) Products not Projects

Created by Peter Gross, last modified on Sep 15, 2020


It is Martin Fowlers belief that microservices should be developed and owned by a single team that is working closely with the business team. 


With traditional projects:

  • The goal is to deliver working code
  • No lasting relationship with the customer
  • Often no acquaintance with the customer
  • After delivering - the team moves onto the next project


The whole process of delivering business functionality and not a layer of technology should lead to closer ties to the actual users of the system.

Martin Fowler is a firm believer that Microservices should align with the agile development methodologies if they are going to work.


With Microservice projects

  • The goal is to deliver a working product
  • A product needs ongoing support and requires close relationship with the customer
  • The team is responsible for the Microservice after the delivery too.


Also AWS CTO is a firm believer that product based teams are best suited to deliver Microservices

So in general the move to microservices is not just a technical excerise, if they are to be a success. 


Motivation

  • Increase the customers satisfaction
  • Change the developers mindset
