Peter gross : Standardised Errors

Created by Peter Gross, last modified on Sep 13, 2020


It should be simple to consume your services, but if each service can define their own format for sending errors back from the calls to their REST api's then you are making the lives of your developers alot more complex.

For example if you have three services that the client can call, but all three have slightly different error formats, then the client has to build this logic into their end for your service.  This is idiotic


From our application we will standardise on the following format

{"errors":[

     {"message":"<Error message>", "field":"<optional field indicator>"}

]}

example expected result from invalid signin fields being entered

           {"errors":[

               {"message":"Email must be valid","field":"email"},

               {"message":"Password must be between 4 and 20 characters","field":"password"}

           ]}

example expected result from invalid signin credentials supplied

           {"errors":[

               {"message":"Invalid credentials"}

           ]}
